Kennedy Center Outings

Hello! Occasionally, I like to go on outings to the Kennedy Center's Millenium Stage, a free performance series. I pick shows based on my fancy and my schedule. Company on these outings is nice, but because of the show's timing is generally hard to come by.

If you've been pointed to this page via an outing announcement I made, it's because I'd like you to know that if that show happens to also matches your fancy and your schedule, you'd have company if you came.

To announce these events to people and get rough RSVP estimates, I have taken to making private Facebook events and to texting people privately. (I make privately-listed Event pages myself because the Kennedy Center doesn't make Facebook Events for this concert series.) This page is to cover logistics that I didn't want to try templating and putting into every outing notice.

Non-oblig.

If I didn't make clear when I invited you to be in this list, I'm happy to invite you, even if you have next to 0 chance of showing up to 99% of the shows. There's no need to feel obligated. I know 6PM on a weekday is impossible for most of us. But if there's that one day... or, more likely, a Sunday... and you end up coming to a show that blows your socks off, I consider that a happy outcome.

Logistics

Cost

The Millenium Stage performances are free.

Timing

Shows start at 6PM. On weeknights, shows run until 7PM, and then the Grand Foyer area has folks walking through for the evening theater shows that start at 7:30. Weekend Millenium shows can run longer.

Sitting groups together can get a bit tricky. The Kennedy Center doesn't let folks save seats for others who haven't arrived. So it helps to be there by 10–15 minutes before the show in order to get a sufficient cluster of seats.

Seats in the back aren't really that bad in terms of audio or visual—there's a huge video display above the stage. But, there's sometimes noise from folks walking around in the Grand Foyer area.

Comms

I've noticed some cellular providers don't have a signal that reaches into the seating area. So, if you need to communicate with folks, it'd be best to send messages by 5:40.

How to get there

Metro

  • Stop at Foggy Bottom.
  • If a pleasant-enough day, the walk is maybe ten minutes, passing the Watergate.
  • If a suboptimal walking day, a free, red shuttle bus is along pretty frequently (I think every 15 minutes), picking up folks at the Foggy Bottom Metro stop.

Parking

I know of folks who've pulled this off, but I don't know how.

Food

Since these shows run 6-7, and some of us don't have time to eat beforehand, light dinner trekking follows the shows. Suggestions for where to go are welcome ahead of time, but otherwise I will try to lead us to decently cheap spots.
